# AltLayer's Rounds

[AltLayer.io](https://altlayer.io/)'s Rounds (1-2) - September 2022 & February 2023

In the latter part of 2022 and early 2023, the Dark Forest universe served as a testing ground for [AltLayer.io](https://altlayer.io/)'s technology through two distinct rounds. These sessions were not just regular gameplay events; they were deployed on the RaaS (Rollup as a Service) chains developed by AltLayer, marking a step in blockchain gaming infrastructure.

The primary objective of these rounds was to conduct a rigorous stress test of AltLayer's RaaS chains. Players were drawn into these trials with the promise of unique rewards: pieces from the [AltLayer OG](https://opensea.io/collection/altlayer-og-badge-nft) and [Oh Ottie!](https://opensea.io/collection/oh-ottie) NFT collections. This incentive structure added an extra layer of excitement and motivation, as participants were not only competing within the game's universe but also contributing to the development of more robust blockchain infrastructure.

While these rounds did not introduce new gameplay mechanics, their significance lay in the underlying technology being tested. Dark Forest has often faced challenges such as spiking gas fees and reverted transactions, which can detract from the player experience. AltLayer.io's initiative used the game as a benchmark to test their services under the strains of a popular, fully onchain game. This move was a vital step towards addressing some of the persistent issues in blockchain and demonstrated how Dark Forest has been instrumental in advancing cryptographic technology.

# DFArchon's DFAres v0.1 Round 1 "Magic Artifacts"

[DFArchon's DFAres v0.1 Round 1 - July 2023](https://paragraph.com/@dfarchon/dark-forest-ares-v01-round-1)

[DFArchon's DFAres v0.1 Magic Artifacts Round Daily Test - September 2023](https://paragraph.com/@dfarchon/dark-forest-ares-v01-magic-artifacts-round-daily-test)

Bringing in an extension to the artifacts within the Dark Forest universe, DFArchon's DFAres v0.1 "Magic Artifacts" round brought a wave of changes to the game's artifact system. This round was notable for introducing new artifacts, each adding a unique strategic element to gameplay, as well as revising existing ones.

Among the fresh introductions was the 'Ice Link' artifact, which opened up the tactical possibility of freezing opponents' planets. This was complemented by its counter, the 'Fire Link,' allowing players to unfreeze their planets, adding a dynamic of offense and defense to the game. In a nod to the original developers' 'Black Domain' artifact, which obliterated planets, the Ice Link and complimentary Fire Link were a more restrained version, balancing destructive power with strategic play.

Further changes were made to existing artifacts like the Photoid Cannon and the Bloom Filter, enhancing their functionality and impact in the game. Additionally, the round addressed the previously [controversial stacking effect](https://x.com/darkforest_eth/status/1497347138648305669) of Motherships and Whales, moving towards a more balanced set of starting ships, where only one of these would be active at a time.

Perhaps one of the more intriguing aspects of this round (particularly for those involved in building onchain games) was the experimentation with in-game financialization. Players were given the ability to purchase artifacts using testnet tokens, albeit with limitations. This move wasn't just about adding a new layer to the game; it was a strategic test for potential financial mechanisms that could support ongoing community development. DFArchon's DFAres v0.1 "Magic Artifacts" round thus stands out as a significant milestone in exploring new ways to enhance gameplay while considering the sustainability and growth of the Dark Forest community.

# DFDAO's Arena Speedruns

DFDAO's [Arena Speedruns](https://arena.dfdao.xyz/portal) - 2024

The latest iteration of DFDAO's 'classic' speedrun competition, originally introduced in 2022, has brought perpetuality to the Dark Forest Community Rounds roster. Unlike previous rounds that typically lasted 1-2 weeks, these bi-weekly events are designed to be an ongoing challenge, adding a new layer of endurance to the game.

One of the most notable changes in this deployment is the decision to host the rounds on Gnosis Mainnet - the original chain upon which Dark Forest ran (and which Lobbies still do). This move was strategic, anticipating the longevity of the chain, expected to endure for years, if not decades. It signifies a shift back towards more 'legitimized' gameplay, where each move carries an inherent transaction cost - whereas recently, many community rounds have been hosted on testnets.

However, this transition to mainnet isn't without its drawbacks. Players now face the unpredictability of fluctuating gas fees, which can lead to delayed or reverted transactions - a critical factor in a game mode where speed is paramount. As the game progresses, with only one map released so far, future map designs might necessitate strategies focused on minimizing moves to keep transaction costs manageable. This would also add an intriguing layer of complexity, making the discovery of the optimal path more challenging.

As players adapt to this new format, it will be fascinating to see how strategies evolve and how the community navigates the balance between speed, cost, and efficiency in this ever-changing landscape.
