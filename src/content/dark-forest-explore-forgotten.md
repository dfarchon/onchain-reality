# The Premise of Dark Forest

At its heart, Dark Forest is a real-time strategy (RTS) game set in universe. Players start by controlling planet, and their goal is to build and expand their empires by capturing other planets, all while competing against others. The game's twist lies in its hidden universe. Much of the game's universe is concealed behind a "fog of war" - a powerful metaphor for the vast unknowns of space. Players can only see regions that are decrypted by GPU or CPU within their exploration computation, which creates an atmosphere of uncertainty and tension.

Inspired by Liu Cixin's _The Three-Body Problem_, the game's title refers to the "Dark Forest Theory". This theory suggests that the universe is a dangerous place where civilizations must remain hidden to avoid destruction. In the game, much of the tension comes from navigating this hidden universe, where an enemy could be right beyond your line of sight preparing for an invasion, or perhaps a planet ripe for expansion lies ahead. It's a balance of strategy and risk, emphasizing exploration and the forgotten unknown behind it.

# The Technology Behind Dark Forest

What sets Dark Forest apart from other games is its innovative use of blockchain technology. Built on EVM compatible and incorporating zk-SNARKs (zero-knowledge succinct non-interactive arguments of knowledge), it's showcases how advanced cryptographic techniques can be applied to gaming in creative ways.

## 1. EVM and Smart Contracts

Dark Forest operates on a blockchain ecosystem, specifically the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM). The game's mechanics and updates are driven by smart contracts, meaning no central authority governs the game. Instead, everything happens in a decentralized manner, and every action can be verified on the blockchain. This marks a significant departure from traditional gaming models, where control typically rests with centralized platforms. Here, the blockchain introduces concepts like true ownership of in-game assets, transparency in mechanics, and a level playing field.

## 2. Zero-Knowledge Proofs

One of the most remarkable aspects of Dark Forest's tech stack is its use of zk-SNARKs. This cryptographic technique allows players to interact with the game while keeping certain details private. For instance, when a player sends energy movement, the exact details remain hidden from others if they doesn't see on their map a source planet of movement. This preserves both the strategic depth of the game and the player's privacy. It's a novel use of zero-knowledge proofs in gaming, enhancing both gameplay and trust in decentralized systems.

## 3. Onchain State and Lazy Update

One challenge of building decentralized games within EVM is scalability. Network may struggle with large-scale games due to current limitations. To address this, Dark Forest uses lazy update mechanism. This allows complex planet state calculations to be simulated off-chain, with forced state updates on the smart contract only when a player performs specific actions on the planet. The lazy update design helps avoid excessive calls, thereby saving RPC resources.

## 4. Open Source and Community Involvement

One of the key features that sets Dark Forest apart is its open-source nature. Developers and enthusiasts from around the world can actively contribute to its codebase, design plugins, and create tools that enhance the gaming experience. This community-driven approach is deeply aligned with the key principles of decentralization and innovation, empowering players and developers alike to shape the evolution of the game.

Several community-led rounds have been initiated based on these foundations, continually pushing forward the development of the game. Our team next development plan is to port Dark Forest's original code, which uses Diamond standard, into the MUD framework. This transition aims to simplify the connection between smart contracts and the game client, allowing developers to focus on game logic rather than the complexity of integrating these layers. By reducing the binding complexities, the community will unlock new experimental possibilities, offering a wider range of opportunities to explore and innovate with today's emerging technologies.

# Lessons Beyond Gaming

Dark Forest transcends the boundaries of gaming, offering valuable insights into the broader world of blockchain technology. The game's mechanics closely mirror real-world challenges in areas such as finance and governance, all of which are beginning to explore the use of zero-knowledge proofs and decentralized architectures. By engaging with Dark Forest, players and developers alike gain hands-on experience with these advanced concepts in a gamified environment, sharpening their understanding of decentralized systems.

Moreover, the game serves as a model for how decentralized virtual worlds might operate as Autonomous Worlds. As digital spaces become more complex and immersive, mastering the ability to manage resources, form unions, and navigate competition will become essential skills. Dark Forest acts as a testing ground for these skills, providing a sandbox environment where players can explore decentralized collaborative innovation.

Additionally, Dark Forest emphasizes the importance of "DYOR" (Do Your Own Research) a principle often highlighted in the blockchain and crypto communities. The game encourages players to continuously learn and adapt, as strategies, technologies, and opportunities evolve rapidly. It teaches that progress, whether in gaming or in the real world, requires curiosity, initiative, and self-education. By diving deeper into the technologies that power the game and experimenting with new ideas, players not only improve their in-game performance but also cultivate valuable skills and knowledge that can be applied far beyond the game, helping them grow both as individuals and professionals.

Dark Forest is more than just a game it's a platform that merges strategic gameplay with advanced cryptographic and decentralized technologies. Whether you're playing to master its strategic depths or exploring the tech behind it, Dark Forest offers a rich environment for learning about blockchain, cryptography, and the future of decentralized systems. As gaming and technology continue to evolve, Dark Forest stands out as a pioneering example of what's possible when these worlds intersect.
